Build America, Buy America (BABA) Compliance

Domestic-content compliance for federally funded infrastructure, from contract provisions to verified supplier documentation.

Overview

The Build America, Buy America Act requires that the iron, steel, manufactured products, and construction materials used on federally funded infrastructure be produced in the United States, unless an approved waiver applies. Proving domestic content takes supplier documentation, careful tracking, and a record that holds up to review.

We review your contract provisions, identify covered materials, collect and verify supplier documentation, track any waivers, and maintain the domestic-content record, so compliance is established and defensible.

What we do

  • Review BABA contract provisions and confirm applicability to the project
  • Identify covered iron, steel, manufactured products, and construction materials
  • Collect and verify supplier and manufacturer domestic-content documentation
  • Track applicable waivers and exceptions and their conditions
  • Close documentation gaps before they become findings
  • Maintain the complete, audit-ready domestic-content record

Who needs it

Public agencies, developers and owners, and general contractors on infrastructure projects subject to BABA domestic-content requirements, including transportation, water, broadband, and energy work.
