Section 3 Compliance — 24 CFR Part 75

Section 3 benchmarks, monitoring, and reporting for HUD-funded projects, with the documentation that proves good-faith effort.

Overview

HUD-funded projects must direct a share of employment, training, and contracting opportunities to Section 3 workers and Section 3 business concerns, and document good-faith effort under 24 CFR Part 75. Meeting the labor-hour benchmarks, and proving it through reporting, takes active management across the life of the project.

We set the benchmarks, guide hiring and training, monitor contractor performance, and prepare the reporting that demonstrates compliance.

What we do

  • Establish Section 3 and Targeted Section 3 labor-hour benchmarks for the project
  • Guide hiring and training toward qualifying Section 3 workers
  • Track Section 3 worker and Targeted Section 3 worker labor hours
  • Monitor contractor and subcontractor compliance throughout the work
  • Prepare and review Section 3 reporting, including HUD SPEARS submissions
  • Maintain good-faith-effort and qualitative-action documentation

Who needs it

Recipients, developers, and contractors on HUD-funded housing and community-development projects subject to Section 3 of the Housing and Urban Development Act.
